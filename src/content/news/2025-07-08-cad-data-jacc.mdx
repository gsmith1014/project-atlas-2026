**TORONTO, July 8, 2025** — CorVista Health announced the publication of pivotal clinical data in *JACC: Cardiovascular Imaging* validating the accuracy of the CorVista System for detecting functionally significant coronary artery disease (CAD) in symptomatic patients at the front line of care.

The study, conducted across 14 clinical sites in the United States and Canada, enrolled 3,241 patients who presented with stable chest discomfort, dyspnea, or unexplained fatigue and were referred for invasive coronary angiography. All patients underwent CorVista System evaluation prior to catheterization, with the CorVista reading blinded to the catheterization result.

### Study design and results

The primary endpoint was sensitivity for obstructive CAD (defined as ≥70% stenosis in a major epicardial vessel or ≥50% in the left main) as confirmed by quantitative coronary angiography.

| Metric | CorVista System | Standard Stress ECG |
|---|---|---|
| Sensitivity | 88% | 61% |
| Specificity | 51% | 71% |
| NPV | 99% | 91% |
| AUC | 0.82 | 0.69 |

The CorVista System's negative predictive value of 99% means that in this population, a negative CorVista result virtually rules out functionally significant obstructive disease — enabling front-line physicians to safely defer referral for the majority of patients presenting with cardiac symptoms.

"The 99% NPV is the number that matters most for clinical practice," said Charles R. Bridges, M.D., Sc.D., EVP and Chief Scientific Officer of CorVista Health. "It gives primary care physicians and cardiologists a high-confidence rule-out they can act on in the same visit — without radiation, without contrast, and without waiting days for a stress lab appointment."

The full publication is available open-access at JACC: Cardiovascular Imaging.
