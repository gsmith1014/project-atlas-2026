For forty years, the front line of cardiovascular diagnosis has looked essentially the same: a resting ECG, a stress test if warranted, and a referral to a cardiologist if the stress test raises concerns. By the time a patient reaches definitive diagnosis, months may have passed — and in many cases, the disease has progressed.

CorVista Health was built to collapse that timeline into a single office visit.

## The diagnostic gap

Cardiovascular disease remains the world's leading cause of death, responsible for nearly 18 million deaths annually. Yet the tools used to detect it at the primary care level have changed little since the 1980s. The standard ECG, for all its ubiquity, misses the majority of functionally significant coronary artery disease. Stress testing is more sensitive but still imprecise, and it cannot screen for pulmonary hypertension — a condition that typically goes undiagnosed for three to five years after symptom onset.

The result is a diagnostic gap: millions of patients with heart disease who pass through routine office visits without receiving a meaningful evaluation.

## What CorVista measures

The CorVista System addresses the gap with a single resting test that assesses for three distinct conditions:

- **Coronary artery disease (CAD):** Functionally significant obstructive disease in stable, symptomatic patients. Sensitivity 88%, specificity 51%, negative predictive value 99%.
- **Pulmonary hypertension (PH):** Elevated pulmonary artery pressure consistent with WHO Group 1 or 2 PH. Sensitivity 82%, specificity 92%, NPV >99%.
- **Elevated pulmonary capillary wedge pressure (PCWP):** Consistent with heart failure with preserved ejection fraction. Sensitivity 82%, specificity 83%, NPV >99%.

All three assessments are generated from the same 220-second resting capture, without radiation, contrast, exercise, or needles.

## How it works at the point of care

A medical assistant places small adhesive sensors on the patient's chest and back — similar to an ECG, but acquiring both electrical and blood-flow signals simultaneously. The patient rests quietly for approximately four minutes. The signals are uploaded to the cloud, processed by AI algorithms trained on more than 10,000 paired catheterization cases, and returned to the ordering physician as a structured report with disease probability scores and recommended next steps.

The full workflow — from patient check-in to report receipt — typically takes less than thirty minutes.

## The clinical impact

For primary care physicians, the system provides something that has never been available before: a front-line rule-out for the three most common serious cardiovascular conditions, delivered within the same visit the patient presents.

For cardiologists, it changes the quality of referrals. Instead of receiving undifferentiated patients from primary care, they receive patients who have already been stratified by disease probability — leading to higher cath lab yield and fewer unnecessary downstream procedures.

Early real-world data suggests the system reduces total CAD diagnostic pathway costs by 32% and PH total cost of care by 44%, by replacing low-yield referral chains with a single informative capture at the front line.
