**TORONTO, March 14, 2026** — CorVista Health announced today that the CorVista System has received CE Mark certification under the EU Medical Device Regulation (EU MDR 2017/745), clearing the path for commercial launch across European markets.

The CE Mark covers assessment of coronary artery disease (CAD) and pulmonary hypertension (PH) in symptomatic patients at the front line of care — consistent with the device's existing FDA 510(k) clearances in the United States.

"European cardiologists and primary care physicians face the same diagnostic bottlenecks as their counterparts in North America," said Charles R. Bridges, M.D., Sc.D., EVP and Chief Scientific Officer of CorVista Health. "CE Mark under the new MDR framework validates the quality of our clinical evidence and opens access to millions of patients who currently wait months for an answer."

The certification follows a comprehensive technical file review under the EU MDR's more stringent conformity assessment requirements, including independent review of the company's clinical investigation data from more than 10,000 patients across 40 clinical sites.

CorVista Health anticipates initial commercial launch in Germany, the United Kingdom, and the Netherlands in the second half of 2026, with broader EU rollout to follow.

## About the CorVista System

The CorVista System captures 220 seconds of resting biosignals using non-invasive sensors, then applies AI algorithms trained on paired catheterization, echocardiography, and right-heart catheterization data to generate disease probability scores. Results are delivered to the ordering clinician as a physician-reviewed report, typically within minutes of acquisition.
