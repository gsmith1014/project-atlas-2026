**CHICAGO / TORONTO, November 30, 2025** — CorVista Health presented updated long-term validation data for its pulmonary hypertension diagnostic algorithm at the American Heart Association Scientific Sessions in Chicago, demonstrating sustained performance across two years of real-world deployment at 28 clinical sites.

The data, presented in a late-breaking clinical trials session, showed that the CorVista PH algorithm maintained sensitivity above 80% and specificity above 90% in a prospective cohort of 1,847 patients evaluated following FDA 510(k) clearance in 2024.

"These results are particularly meaningful because they come from real clinical settings — community hospitals, cardiology practices, and primary care offices — not controlled research environments," said Shyam Ramchandani, Ph.D., MBA, VP of Research at CorVista Health. "Performance held up across patient populations, geographic regions, and clinical workflows."

The presented analysis also included sub-group data across patient demographics, comorbidity profiles, and referring specialty — all of which showed consistent algorithm performance. The company noted that no material degradation in model performance was observed over the two-year post-clearance period, consistent with the company's continuous validation program.

### Key findings presented

- Sensitivity: 83% (95% CI: 80–86%) across the prospective cohort
- Specificity: 91% (95% CI: 89–93%)
- Negative predictive value: >99% across all sub-groups evaluated
- Time from test to report: median 7 minutes across all sites

The data will be submitted for peer-reviewed publication. The full presentation is available to registered AHA Scientific Sessions attendees through the conference archive.
